Quick Summary

VoidPanel respects your privacy. We collect data required to register accounts, provision web hosting databases, secure logins, process payments via Razorpay, and improve service delivery. We do not sell your personal data.

1. Information We Collect

We collect information you provide directly to us, as well as data automatically generated when you interact with our websites and services.

  • Account Information: When registering, we collect your name, email address, password, phone number, and billing/mailing address.
  • Payment & Billing Data: Payment details (e.g. transactions, card types) are transmitted securely using encryption protocols to third-party payment gateways. We do not store raw card numbers on our server databases.
  • Service-Related Data: Domain registration inputs, nameserver choices, email configuration parameters, and SSL certificate domains you manage via our client portal.
  • System & Network Logs: System information, browser profiles, IP addresses, log data from your terminal accesses, API usage metrics, and error logs collected for system diagnostics.

2. How We Use Information

We process customer information solely to fulfill service agreements, ensure platform security, and improve VoidPanel tools.

  1. Service Provisioning: Setting up databases, configuring custom domains, securing custom professional mailboxes, and activating premium hosting plans.
  2. Support & Communication: Responding to client billing queries, managing active support tickets, and issuing email notifications about critical system updates or service expirations.
  3. Platform Security: Monitoring logins, securing database access, preventing privilege attacks in terminal shells, and conducting fraud checks.
  4. Aggregated Improvements: Analyzing website traffic patterns via Google Analytics to optimize page load speeds and structure documentation.

3. Data Protection & Safety

We implement robust organizational and technical measures to shield data against unauthorized access, leaks, alteration, or deletion.

  • Encryption: Password credentials are salted and hashed. All traffic between your browser, host servers, and client portal is encrypted using secure Transport Layer Security (TLS/HTTPS).
  • System Isolation: Within the VoidPanel host node setups, user home directories are strictly isolated using Unix permission modes (e.g., 750/711 configurations) to prevent local traversal across account nodes.
  • Terminal Scoping: System terminal sessions are restricted to authorized usernames and home directories, blocking access to administrative root directories.

4. Third-Party Service Providers

We may share your data with selected external service providers only as necessary to deliver services on our behalf:

  • Payment Gateway (Razorpay): Processes payments securely. Their database handles card authorizations according to PCI-DSS standards.
  • Domain Registrars: Transmits registrant details to WHOIS databases and registry APIs to secure ownership of domain names.
  • Analytics: Google Analytics collects anonymous website metrics to evaluate documentation coverage.

5. Cookies & Tracking

VoidPanel uses cookies and standard browser local storage mechanism to optimize your dashboard and login states:

Cookies store active sessions, CSRF security tokens to prevent cross-site request forgery, and billing cart selections. You may disable cookies in your browser settings, but doing so will prevent you from signing in to the billing portal or finalizing orders.

6. GDPR & CCPA Compliance Rights

Depending on your geographic location, you possess standard data rights under local legislation (such as the GDPR or CCPA):

  • Right of Access: Request a copy of all personal information we store about your customer account.
  • Right to Rectification: Edit or correct inaccurate email details, client tags, or billing information directly inside the portal.
  • Right to Erasure (“Right to be Forgotten”): Request the permanent deletion of your client billing profile, billing transactions, and provisioned hosting servers, subject to legal bookkeeping requirements.
  • Right to Portability: Obtain a structured, machine-readable copy of your personal data.
